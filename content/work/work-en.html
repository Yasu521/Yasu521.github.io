Development of Clinical Trial Success Prediction Model

Background

Drug development takes over 10 years and costs billions of dollars, with success rates dropping from 1 in 15,000 a decade ago to 1 in 30,000 today. Streamlining trials and optimizing resources have been long-standing industry challenges, especially with the Phase II to Phase III success rate being only 24.6%.

Challenge

We tackled the prediction of success probabilities for Phase III clinical trials of immune checkpoint inhibitors. By applying the world model's concept of "learning representation from observations," we visualized patient blood and vital data to enhance accuracy.

Tasks

1. Translating abstract concepts like immune environments into concrete, actionable processes.
2. Implementing AI in healthcare projects addressing societal issues over the next decade.
