Matsuo Iwasawa Lab

2023 - 2025

Developed a machine learning model to predict forgetting timing and improve learning efficiency for elementary and middle school students using tablet-based educational materials. Achieved optimal review scheduling by leveraging student-specific features.

In the Matsuo-Iwasawa Laboratory, I developed a machine learning model that accurately predicts when elementary and middle school students are likely to forget kanji, English vocabulary, or arithmetic and provides review questions at the optimal time, contributing to the improvement of their fundamental academic skills.
Conventional digital learning materials have presented review questions at uniform intervals, regardless of individual differences in students' strengths, weaknesses, or the timing of memory decay, leaving room for improvement in learning efficiency.
To address this, the model incorporates three types of features: "student-specific features," such as individual attributes and past learning history; "problem-specific features," such as the characteristics and difficulty of each question; and "student-problem interaction features," which capture unique handwriting patterns for each question through deep learning. By leveraging these features, the model enables optimal review scheduling based on an AI-estimated forgetting curve tailored to each student.
